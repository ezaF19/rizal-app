Consuelo Ortiga

Consuelo Ortiga y Rey, the prettier of Don Pablo Ortiga’s daughters, fell in love with him. He dedicated to her A la Senorita C.O. y R., which became one of his best poems.

Gertrude Beckett

While Rizal was in London annotating the Sucesos de las Islas Filipinas. Gertrude,a blue-eyed and buxom girl was the oldest of the three Beckett daughters. She fell in love with Rizal.

Josephine Bracken

Rizal met an 18-year old petite Irish girl. She was Josephine Bracken who came to Dapitan to seek Rizal for eye treatment.

Leonor Rivera

Leonor Rivera, his sweetheart for 11 years. Sadly, consented to marry the Englishman Henry Kipping her mother’s choice.

Nellie Boustead

While a guest of the Boustead family at their residence in the resort city of Biarritz, he had befriended the two pretty daughters of his host, Eduardo Boustead

O Sei San

O Sei San taught Rizal the Japanese art of painting known as su-mie. If Rizal was a man without a patriotic mission, he would have married this lovely and intelligent woman

Segunda Katigbak

Segunda Katigbak was his puppy love. Unfortunately, his first love was engaged to be married to a town mate- Manuel Luz

Suzanne Jacoby

In 1890, Rizal moved to Brussels. He lived in the boarding house of the two Jacoby sisters. Suzanne cried when Rizal left Brussels and wrote him when he was in Madrid.

Leonor Valenzuela

a tall girl from Pagsanjan. Rizal send her love notes written in invisible ink, that could only be deciphered over the warmth of the lamp or candle.
