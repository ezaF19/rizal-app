José Protasio Rizal Mercado Y Alonso Realonda


June 19, 1861 - December 30, 1896

Patriot, physician, martyr and national hero of the Malayan race.

Born in Calamba, Philippines. While living in Europe, Rizal wrote about the discrimination that accompanied Spain's colonial rule of his country. He returned to the Philippines in 1892, but was exiled due to his desire for reform. Although he supported peaceful change, Rizal was convicted of sedition and executed on Bagumbayan Field at the age of 35.

  • 1861-1872

    First Period

    In José Rizal's childhood, he learned how to read, write by listen to the stories and teachings of his mother, Teodora Alonzo Y Quintos, whom have also encouraged his son in expressing sentiments and ideas in verses. He was also able to write a poem about the love of one's native language entitled "Sa Aking Mga Kabata" at the age of eight.

  • 1872-1882

    Second Period

    The first turning point in the life of Rizal, when at 11 years old, was enrolled at Ateneo Municipal. It was the period when Frs. Gomez, Burgos and Zamora were unjustly executed by the Spanish government.

  • 1882-1892

    Third Period

    This is the time that Rizal decided to leave the Philippines to escape persecution. He went to Europe and became an observer and a student.He urged the Filipino colony in Spain to prove that Filipinos can compete with the Europeans in intellect and talent. It is also during this period that he took part in Propaganda Movement, which is based in Europe.

  • 1892-1896

    Fourth Period

    The last period in the life of Rizal. Rizal was exiled in Dapitan, where he also demonstrated what an individual can do and accomplish within a short period of time. It was here where he detached his connection with politics and devoted more of his time in practical service and usefulness to the community.
